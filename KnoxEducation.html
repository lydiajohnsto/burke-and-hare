Dr. Robert Knox

 

Education

 

While at the Royal High School, Dr. Knox was a gold medalist and in his final year, the top pupil in his school. In 1810 when he went on to study medicine at the University of Edinburgh, he studied anatomy under the respected anatomist Dr. John Barclay. Dr. John Barclay is said to have considered Dr. Knox as his most brilliant student. He graduated with his MD from the University of Edinburgh in 1814. In 1815 while serving in Waterloo, he learned surgery fast as there was only one surgeon and one assistant per regiment. During this time, he also gained experience with dissection.
