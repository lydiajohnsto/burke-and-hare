November 29, 1827

Donald, a lodger in Hare's house, dies of dropsy. Burke and Hare sell Donald's body to Dr. Knox at Edinburgh University for £7 10s, marking their first body sale.

 

January-February, 1828

Joseph, a miller and lodger, smothered by Hare with Burke's assistance. Body sold to Knox for £10.

Either Abigail Simpson, a salt seller, or an unnamed English male lodger suffering from jaundice. Both were murdered using the same suffocation method and sold to Knox for £10 each.

 

February-March, 1828

An old woman invited by Margaret Hare, killed by suffocation after being intoxicated. Sold for £10 to Knox.

 

April, 1828

Burke lures Mary Paterson and her friend Janet Brown to his brother’s house. After a domestic altercation, Mary is killed while asleep. Her body is sold to Knox for £8, still warm.

 

Mid 1828

A stout old woman, Mrs. Haldane, lodges at Hare’s house, becomes intoxicated, and is killed. Body sold to Knox.

Margaret Haldane, the daughter of Mrs. Haldane. Burke kills her alone after drinking heavily. Body sold to Knox for £8.

Effy, a scavenger, enticed with whisky and killed. Body sold for £10.

Burke helps an intoxicated, unknown woman to Hare’s house, kills her, and sells her body for £10.

 

June, 1828

An unknown woman and her grandson, both lodgers, the grandmother is killed first, followed by her grandson. Bodies sold for £8 each after being forced into a herring barrel.

 

July, 1828

Hare secretly murders an unknown woman while Burke is in Falkirk. Burke discovers this and accuses Hare, leading to a brief fallout between the two men.

 

September-October, 1828

Mrs. Ostler, a washerwoman, intoxicated and murdered. Body sold for £8

Ann McDougal, a relative of Helen McDougal, killed and sold for £10.

James Wilson, also known as "Daft Jamie." A well-known figure in Edinburgh, lured by Hare and killed after a struggle. Body sold for £10. Recognized by Knox’s students, who are dismissed by Knox.

 

October 31, 1828

Margery Docherty, Lured by Burke under the pretense of shared Irish roots, she is murdered after heavy drinking. Her body is discovered hidden in straw by the Grays, who alert the police. Burke, Hare, and their accomplices are arrested.
